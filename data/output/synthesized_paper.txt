**Automatic Speaker Verification Spoofing and Deepfake Detection: A Synthesis of Prior Work**

**Abstract**

This paper synthesizes key findings from recent research on automatic speaker verification spoofing and deepfake detection. The primary contributions of this synthesis are the identification of improved generalisation and domain robustness using pre-trained, self-supervised speech models, and the complementary benefits of data augmentation and self-attention based aggregation layers. Additionally, the analysis highlights the importance of capturing local up-sampling artifacts in deepfake detection.

**Methods**

This synthesis draws upon prior work in the field of automatic speaker verification spoofing and deepfake detection. Key findings are extracted from multiple research papers, including the use of pre-trained, self-supervised speech models, data augmentation, and self-attention based aggregation layers. The analysis also considers the role of up-sampling operations in deepfake detection and the introduction of neighboring pixel relationships (NPR) as a means to capture and characterize generalized structural artifacts.

**Results**

The synthesis reveals that pre-trained, self-supervised speech models with fine-tuning demonstrate improved generalisation and domain robustness. Data augmentation is shown to provide complementary benefits to self-supervised learning, and the introduction of self-attention based aggregation layers brings additional improvements. Furthermore, the analysis highlights the importance of capturing local up-sampling artifacts in deepfake detection, with the introduction of neighboring pixel relationships (NPR) achieving state-of-the-art performance.
