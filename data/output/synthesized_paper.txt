**Rethinking the Up-Sampling Operations in CNN-Based Generative Network for Generalizable Deepfake Detection**

**Abstract**

Deepfake detection has become a pressing concern in the era of deep generative models. Recent studies have demonstrated the effectiveness of CNN-based generative networks in detecting deepfakes. However, the up-sampling operations in these networks can introduce generalized structural artifacts, compromising the detection performance. This paper proposes a novel approach to address this issue by introducing a simple yet effective artifact representation, termed neighboring pixel relationships (NPR). Our method showcases a remarkable improvement over existing methods, achieving state-of-the-art performance in deepfake detection.

**Methods**

Our approach builds upon the observation that up-sampling operators can cause generalized forgery artifacts beyond frequency-based artifacts. We introduce a novel artifact representation, NPR, designed to capture local up-sampling artifacts from image pixels. Our method is implemented using a lightweight CNN network, leveraging convolutional layers and ResNet blocks as classifiers for NPR. We employ PyTorch on an NVIDIA GeForce RTX 3090 GPU to implement our method.

**Results**

Our results demonstrate that NPR outperforms its counterparts, showcasing higher mean accuracy and comparable mean average precision metrics. We achieve state-of-the-art performance on the diffusionforensics dataset, outperforming existing methods by 7.1% and 20.9% in terms of mean accuracy. Our method also exhibits better transferability than most existing methods.
